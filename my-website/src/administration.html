Administration

The Minerals Development Fund (MDF) was established by the Minerals Development Fund Act, 2016 (Act 912) to provide financial resources to the mining community for sustainable development. The Fund is also mandated to promote the development of the mineral resources of Ghana and to ensure that the benefits of mineral resources accrue to the people of Ghana.

Key Functions

OFFICE OF THE ADMINISTRATOR

Achieve MDF goals through strategic planning, implementation, management, and reporting of its affairs to the Board and day-to-day coordination of its activities.

SCHEME COORDINATION

Provide technical and administrative support for the achievement of the objectives of the Mining Community Development Scheme.

ADMINISTRATION

Advise and supervise the implementation of administrative policies and support services to facilitate the work of MDF.

FINANCE

Provide technical, operational, and administrative leadership for effective financial management for the achievement of the objectives of the Secretariat.

RESEARCH

Provide advisory support on research-related projects for effective funds management for the achievement of the objectives of the Secretariat.

PUBLIC RELATIONS

Provide leadership and communication strategy for efficient management and maintenance of a good image and reputation of the institute.

Our Structure Explained

Board Level

The Minerals Development Fund (MDF) Board sits at the apex of our organization, providing strategic direction and oversight for all operations.

Administrator

The Administrator serves as the chief executive, responsible for implementing board policies and managing day-to-day operations.

Support Services

Client Services, Public Relations, Internal Audit, and Procurement form our essential support services, ensuring efficient operations and transparency.

Core Operations

General Administration and Operations divisions manage our key functions, including HR, Finance, Standards, and Community Development.

Implementation Units

Specialized units like Revenue Management, Community Development Coordination, and M&E Compliance ensure effective program delivery and monitoring.
